Fumo Resellers and Storefronts

Spotting Bootlegs

All official Fumos originally came with either a tag or a tin badge. If that is missing, that could be a sign of a bootleg (although it's possible the seller lost the tag or badge, or bought it from someone else who originally lost the tag or badge). A missing tag will generally lower the price too.

Keep in mind that Fumos originally sell for around $40 (or around $500 for a Deka). If you see a price lower than that, it's very likely to be a bootleg or other fraudulent listing.

Look at the photos in the listing. Try reverse image searching them to make sure they're unique. If the Fumo looks particularly derpy, it's probably a bootleg. You can compare against the stock photos in our Fumo List.

There are some bootleg Deka Fumos with very long legs (the originals have the same stubby legs as their smaller counterparts).

Commonly Used Resellers and Storefronts

    This is a list of sites used by independent Fumo sellers.

  • Yahoo Auctions JP
    • Basically Japanese eBay. Every proxy can interact with it.
    • Always has Fumos listed, and mostly at reasonable prices.
    • Just like eBay, you can be sniped or outbid.
    • Some sellers have blacklisted some proxies.

  • Mercari
    • No auctions, just direct sales.
    • Often has individual and group batches of fumos.
    • Proxies can be too slow for some of the quicker deals.

  • Rakuten
    • A storefront for both corporations and individual sellers.
    • When searching, use 東方ぬいぐるみ (Touhou Plush) not 東方ふもふも (Touhou Fumo).
    • Prices might be fair for newer Fumos, but are very expensive for older ones.

    This is a list of common and/or popular online stores that often have Fumos in stock.

  • Gift Online Shop
    • The source of all Fumos. Prices are always cheapest here.
    • The most popular Fumos tend to sell out in an hour or less.
    • Proxies are often too slow, or sometimes reach order limits.
    • Since August 2021, Gift has preferred AmiAmi over its own storefront for fumo releases.

  • Amiami
    • Since August 2021, Gift has been officially releasing fumos on AmiAmi.
    • Offers shipping through DHL, EMS, and surface mail to most international locations.
    • AmiAmi's site tends to experience reliability issues when traffic is high (i.e., around starting time of fumo sales).
    • There is a separate Japanese storefront that only ships to addresses inside Japan.
    • Warning: The Japanese storefront has been rumored to reject orders that would ship to known forwarders.

  • Suruga-ya
    • Second-hand retailer of lots of hobby goods.
    • Will nearly always have Fumos in stock.
    • Prices vary wildly but tend to be more expensive than Gift's.
    • Also has an English storefront.

  • Mandarake
    • Sells preowned hobby goods.
    • Fumos here tend to sell very quickly.
    • This is a digital storefront for physical stores, so staff has to confirm that nobody has bought your item before you can pay.

  • Amazon Japan
    • It's Amazon but in Japanese.
    • Fumos are rarely listed here and are usually very expensive.

  • SolarisJapan
    • English storefront for Japanese figures and merchandise.
    • Prices are very high.

  • Goods Republic
    • English storefront for Japanese figures and merchandise.
    • Prices are very high.
    • Although the site is in English, searches still need to be in Japanese.

  • JPFigures
    • Literally a scam. Do not purchase anything from them.
    • They have a nice looking webpage. Don't be fooled.
    • The link above is a rickroll.
